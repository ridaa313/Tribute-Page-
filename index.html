Quaid-e-Azam Muhammad Ali Jinnah

Early Life and Education

Quaid-e-Azam Muhammad Ali Jinnah's early education began at the Sindh Madrasatul Islam in Karachi, where he studied from 1887 to 1892. He later attended the Christian Mission School in Karachi. At the age of 16, Jinnah traveled to England to study law at Lincoln's Inn, London, where he became a barrister-at-law in 1896.

Career and Achievements

Quaid-e-Azam Muhammad Ali Jinnah's illustrious career spanned over four decades, during which he played a pivotal role in the Pakistan Movement. He became a member of the Indian National Congress in 1906 and later joined the All-India Muslim League in 1913. Jinnah's exceptional leadership skills and unwavering commitment to the cause of Muslim rights led to his appointment as the President of the Muslim League in 1936. He successfully negotiated the Lahore Resolution in 1940, which called for a separate homeland for Muslims. His relentless efforts ultimately led to the creation of Pakistan on August 14, 1947, with Jinnah as its first Governor-General.
